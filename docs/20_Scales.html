1 Calculating scales

In the following section we calculate the scales used in our survey.

1.1 Big Five

An exploratory factor analysis using MinRes shows, that the OCEN scales work well. However, the A scale seems to be confounded with answering tendency (skewd towards answering with agree). This is however, also explainable since agreebleness would measure how much people answer “agree” on a survey.

1.2 Scale Reliability Big Five

The reliability of our scales vary from:

  • openness Cronbach’s \(\alpha = 0.339\),
  • conscientiousness \(\alpha = 0.395\),
  • extraversion \(\alpha = 0.565\),
  • agreeableness \(\alpha = 0.048\),
  • and neuroticism \(\alpha = 0.502\).
